Submission Instructions

We solicit two categories of papers.

Workshop papers: describing new, previously unpublished research in this field. The submissions should follow the ICML style guidelines. We accept both short (4 pages of content) and long papers (8 pages of content). Submissions will be subject to a double-blind review process (i.e. they need to be anonymized). Final versions of accepted papers will be allowed 1 additional page of content so that reviewer comments can be taken into account.

Published papers: papers on topics relevant to the workshop theme, previously published at ML conferences. These papers can be submitted in their original format without hiding the author names. Submissions will be reviewed for fit to the workshop topics.

In both categories, accepted papers will be:
  • can be non-archival
  • published on the workshop website
  • presented at the workshop as a lightning talk

Cross-submission Policy: As long as it doesn't conflict with the cross-submission policies of the other venue (e.g., the ARR policy), you may submit the paper to ToM as a regular workshop paper. Please feel free to email us if you are not sure about your case!
